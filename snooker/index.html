Snooker


I started playing cue sports a lot when I was 15. At the time, I had a lot of fun just potting balls (shooting balls into holes), and for the amateur player potting a ball is probably the most satisfying feeling of playing. Potting balls, however, didn't turn cue sports into a passion for me. My passion comes from the strategy and underlying mechanics, which often go unnoticed by beginners.
There are a lot of different cue sports but the same mechanics can be applied to every game. So, mechanics are the same but every game has its own rules which require different strategies. The game I'm most passionate about, and talking about, is Snooker.

The Mechanics and The Play

Being able to pot balls is important, every beginner should practice just potting balls. After learning to consistently pot balls, the next step (which is a big step) is learning to set up the next shot. This is what separates the professionals from the amateurs. In other words, the next shot can be really tough, like a tight angled shot, or really easy, like a dead-straight shot. Setting up is all about striking the cue ball (the white ball) to give it momentum and rotation.
